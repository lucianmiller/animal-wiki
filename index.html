Argentine Black and White Tegu

The argentine black and white tegu is a species of tegu lizard that lives in South America. It is the largest species of tegu, growing 4.0-4.5 ft long and weighing up to 5.5-15.4 lbs, with the males being bigger. They are omnivorous, eating both vegetation and meat. This species of tegu are kept as pets and are often explained as dog like if properly socialized. However, they are difficult lizards to keep because of their size and needs, and if not properly socialized can be very aggressive and territorial, and should only be kept by those prepared for their needs.

American Kestrel

The American Kestrel is the smallest and most common species of Falcon in North America. Depending on the sex, the Kestrel can range from the size and weight of a Blue Jay to a Morning dove, with females being the larger of the two sexes. They are ambush predators, mostly eating insects, small lizards, mice and small birds. The American Kestrel is a common bird used in Falconry, a sport which uses Raptors to hunt. They are one of two birds beginner Falconers are able to use. The other being Red-tailed Hawks.

Maned Wolf

The Maned Wolf is a large canine located in South America. Weighing 44-66 lbs and being about 3 feet tall, the maned wolf is the biggest canine in South America. Maned wolves are omnivorous, eating both small and medium sized prey items, such as small mammals, birds and fish. They also eat plant matter like sugarcane, tubers and fruit. Despite having wolf in the name, the maned wolf is not a species of wolf at all, and is in fact the only species in it's genus, Chrysocyon (meaning 'golden dog').
